ABSTRACT

Waves are fundamental phenomena that play an important role in the transfer of energy and information without the permanent movement of matter. They occur in many forms, including mechanical waves such as sound and water waves, and electromagnetic waves such as light, radio waves, and x-rays. This study explores the basic properties of waves, including wavelength, frequency, amplitude, and wave speed, and explains how the properties influence wave behavior.

INTRODUCTION

A wave is any disturbance that transfer energy from one medium to another medium without the transfer of matter. They are observed in everyday life such as ocean waves, sound from musical instruments, light from the sun.

TYPES OF WAVES


LONGITUDINAL WAVES: they are waves in which the particles of the medium vibrate parallel to the direction of wave travel. In these waves, they are compressions and rarefactions

A transverse wave is a type of wave in which the particles of the medium vibrate perpendicular to the direction in which the wave is travelling.

WAVES PROPERTIES

AMPLITUDE:


The maximum displacement of a point from its mean position

FREQUENCY:


The number of complete oscillations make per cycle

WAVELENGTH:


The distance between two successive crest or trough

PERIOD:


The time taken to complete one oscillation
